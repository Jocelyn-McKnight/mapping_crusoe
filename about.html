The Origin

The Mapping Robinson Crusoe Project was created for the final project of the English 4400: Violent Voyages course at the University of Lethbridge Fall 2025. The Creator, Jocelyn McKnight, Came to the idea when thinking about movement in Robinson Crusoe and other GIS/Digital Mapping projects. Particularly, the Diogenet project, which mapped out the movements of Greek philosophers, played a big inspiration. Unfortunately, this project is no longer active but it used to be found at https://diogenet.ucsd.edu/map/. Originally, the idea behind the Crusoe project was to replicate the the Diogenet Project but here we were looking for the relationship between fictional and factual text. The project evolved from the original idea, as fortunately, there is a debate in Crusoe studies on the texts that could have inspired Robinson Crusoe. The mapping project was the perfect way to get involved in the debate and use computational tools to provide more evidence.
